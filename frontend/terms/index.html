Biometric Data and Face Verification

Optional Feature

VoteStack offers an optional biometric face verification feature. Election organizers may choose to enable this feature for their elections. It is disabled by default and must be explicitly enabled per election.

How It Works

When face verification is enabled for an election:

  • The election organizer enrolls a reference photo of each voter
  • Reference photos are processed immediately — a mathematical face embedding (a 512-dimensional vector) is generated and stored
  • The original photos are never stored on our servers; only the mathematical embedding is retained
  • At voting time, a live camera capture is compared against the stored embedding using cosine similarity
  • Liveness detection (blink/head movement) is applied to prevent static photo spoofing

Organizer Responsibilities

If you enable face verification as an election organizer, you:

  • Must obtain explicit written consent from each voter before enrolling their biometric data
  • Are responsible for informing voters how their biometric data will be used
  • Must comply with applicable biometric privacy laws (e.g. BIPA, GDPR Article 9)
  • Acknowledge that embeddings are stored encrypted in your election's database records
  • Can delete voter embeddings at any time by removing the voter from your election

Voter Rights

As a voter subject to face verification:

  • You may refuse face verification and should contact your election organizer to discuss alternatives
  • Your biometric embedding is used solely to verify your identity for this election
  • Embeddings are not shared with third parties or used for any other purpose
  • You may request deletion of your biometric data by contacting the election organizer
